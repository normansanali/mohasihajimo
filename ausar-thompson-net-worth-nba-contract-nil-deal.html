NBA Contract &amp; NIL Deal

As of 2023, Ausar Thompson, a guard for the Detroit Pistons, has an estimated net worth of $5 million.

The net worth of Ausar Thompson is largely contributed by his NBA contract and NIL deal.

He is the twin brother of basketball player Amen Thompson who was selected 4th overall in the 2023 NBA Draft by the Houston Rockets. 

Ausar was selected 5th overall in the 2023 NBA Draft by the Detroit Pistons. 

They became the first-ever pair of brothers to be selected in the same year’s top 5 picks of the NBA Draft. This unprecedented achievement showcases their exceptional basketball skills and sets a new standard for sibling duos in the league.

On July 2, 2023, Ausar Thompson signed a four-year, $36.25 million rookie scale contract with the Pistons.

This contract includes a $36.24 million signing bonus and $36.24 million guaranteed.

In 2023-24, Thompson will earn a base salary of $7.97 million while carrying a cap hit of $7.97 million and a dead cap value of $7.97 million.

Despite Ausar Thompson‘s impressive talent and rankings, his current NIL deal stands at $67K, which may come as a surprise.

In high school, he held a respectable basketball NIL Rank of 68th and garnered a significant following on Instagram, with his account currently boasting 224K followers.

However, it seems that Ausar chose a different approach than most players after high school.

He likely prioritized his career path to the NBA, dedicating his efforts to honing his skills rather than actively building his personal brand at the time.

A Brief On Ausar Thompson

Ausar Thompson’s basketball journey began at Pine Crest School in Fort Lauderdale, Florida, where he quickly established himself as a standout player.

His exceptional skills and performances earned him a five-star recruit rating and a pivotal role in securing a state title for his team.

However, his talent and ambition propelled him to make an unconventional decision. He opted to forgo his senior year of high school and instead signed with Overtime Elite (OTE), an elite basketball development program.

This bold move demonstrated his dedication to his career path and pursuit of excellence.

During his time with OTE, Ausar’s exceptional abilities shone brightly. He played two seasons with the program, achieving remarkable success.

Not only did he contribute significantly to his team’s consecutive OTE titles, but he also earned the prestigious honor of being named the Finals Most Valuable Player (MVP) twice.

Additionally, his outstanding performances throughout the 2023 season led him to be crowned league MVP, solidifying his status as a rising star in basketball.
