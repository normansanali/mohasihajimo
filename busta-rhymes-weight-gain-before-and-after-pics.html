Trevor George Smith Jr., professionally known as Busta Rhymes, is an American rapper, songwriter, record producer, and actor.

He was given the name Busta Rhymes by Chuck D of Public Enemy, inspired by George “Buster” Rhymes, a wide receiver in the NFL and CFL.

Despite receiving 12 Grammy Award nominations for his work, Busta Rhymes has yet to win, making him one of the most-nominated artists without a win.

The rapper and songwriter initially gained prominence as a member of Leaders of the New School, a group that garnered national attention when they toured with Public Enemy.

His breakthrough moment came when he made a guest appearance on A Tribe Called Quest’s track “Scenario,” which further boosted his visibility and popularity.

Busta Rhymes Weight Gain 

According to Busta, he experienced weight gain after the deaths of two important individuals in his life, namely his father, Trevor Smith, and his longtime manager and friend, Chris Lighty.

Their absence made the renowned rapper and musician feel deprived, as he wanted them to witness his success.

Consequently, he neglected his diet and exercise routine. However, a significant event in early 2019 served as a turning point for him.

After a night of partying, Busta was discovered asleep in his car, and his son, upon realizing his father’s difficulty breathing, became concerned and sought help.

It took approximately 20 minutes for his son and the security team to awaken him and bring him inside.

Following a consultation with a throat specialist, it was determined that the musician had polyps in his throat, which severely restricted his ability to breathe, with approximately 90 percent of his air passage blocked. Consequently, he had to undergo emergency surgery to address the issue.
