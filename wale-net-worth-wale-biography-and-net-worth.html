Wale Biography And Career

Born in Washington, D.C., Wale, also known as Olubowale Victor Akintimehin, originally pursued a career in football, and attended college on a football scholarship. He subsequently chose to drop out and focus on music, and began to make a name for himself in the mid-2000s.

His first single to receive radio play was 2006’s, “Rhyme of the Century”. He released his first mixtape the same year and began to attract the attention of publications like The Source and The Washington Post. By the release of his third mixtape in 2007, he had been named “D.C. Metro Breakthrough Artist of the Year”, caught the attention of Fader Magazine, and toured as part of Mark Ronson’s UK Tour.

He signed to both Interscope and Allido Records in 2008. His debut studio album, “Attention Deficit”, dropped in 2009 and reached #21 on the Billboard Hot 200. His second album “Ambition” was released in November 2011. He released a third album in 2013 titled The Gifted which reached #1 on the Billboard 200 as well as on the US R&B and US Rap charts and #10 in Canada. Wale’s fourth album The Album About Nothing was released in 2015 and also reached #1 on the Billboard 200, US R&B, and US Rap charts.

He released the album Shine in 2017. Wale’s single “Lotus Flower Bomb” (featuring Miguel) reached #1 on the US R&B chart and he has also had success with the singles “Chillin” (featuring Lady Gaga), “That Way” (featuring Jeremih and Rick Ross), “Bag of Money” (featuring Rick Ross, Meek Mill, and T-Pain), “Bad” (featuring Tiara Thomas or Rihanna), “Ride Out” (with Kid Ink, Tyga, YG, and Rich Homie Quan), and more. Wale has won BET Awards and been nominated for a Grammy Award for Best Rap Song for “Lotus Flower Bomb”.

Personal life

An avid professional wrestling fan, Wale has made several wrestling references on his songs. Wale hosts the annual WaleMania during WrestleMania weekend. He has also made several appearances on WWE television, where he hosted a rap battle between The New Day and The Usos on the 4 July episode of Smackdown Live in 2017, and was the guest commentator during Noam Dar and TJP’s match on 205 Live in 2018. On October 2, 2019, Wale escorted former NXT Tag Team championsThe Street Profits to the ring for their title shot versus champions Bobby Fish and Kyle O’Reilly.
